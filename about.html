Our History

Founded in 1869, the Yale School of Art has a legacy of excellence in art education. Over the years, our alumni have become leaders in the world of contemporary art, shaping culture and pushing boundaries. We are proud of our rich history and its influence on the global art scene.

Our Mission

At Yale School of Art, we are committed to empowering artists to challenge conventions, explore new ideas, and create work that can inspire change. Our programs are designed to foster intellectual growth, creativity, and collaboration, offering students the opportunity to become visionaries in the field of art.

Core Values

  • Artistic Excellence
  • Diversity and Inclusion
  • Innovation and Experimentation
  • Community and Collaboration

Academic Programs

The Yale School of Art offers a range of graduate programs designed to foster a deep understanding of the arts and develop innovative, creative skills. We offer programs in Fine Art, Graphic Design, Painting, Sculpture, Photography, Printmaking, and more.

Notable Alumni

Our alumni have gone on to achieve great success in the art world. Some notable graduates include:

  • Richard Serra - Renowned Sculptor
  • Chuck Close - Famous Portrait Artist
  • Julie Mehretu - Influential Contemporary Artist
  • Tyson Chandler - Artist and Activist

State-of-the-Art Facilities

The Yale School of Art is equipped with cutting-edge facilities to support the development of our students' artistic practices. From spacious studios to advanced equipment for digital arts and photography, our resources ensure that students have the best possible environment to excel in their creative endeavors.

Global Impact

The Yale School of Art has a far-reaching impact on the global art community. Our students and faculty engage with art and culture from around the world, and our exhibitions, lectures, and partnerships help promote international dialogue and understanding.
