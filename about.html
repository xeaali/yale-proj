Our History

Since its founding in 1869, Yale School of Art has cultivated generations of influential artists. Our legacy continues to shape the global art scene through our alumni and faculty.

Our Mission

We empower artists to challenge the ordinary, push boundaries, and lead cultural dialogue. Our programs emphasize experimentation, diversity, and excellence in all artistic disciplines.

Academic Programs

We offer intensive MFA programs in Painting, Sculpture, Photography, Graphic Design, and more. Each program combines theory, practice, and mentorship to nurture artistic voices.

Notable Alumni

Our alumni continue to influence the world of contemporary art. A few distinguished graduates include:

Richard Serra

Sculptor

Richard Serra is an American sculptor known for working with large-scale installations and steel. His work explores mass and space, often challenging the viewer's perception.

Chuck Close

Portrait Artist

Chuck Close is an American painter and photographer best known for his massive-scale portraits. His technique and use of grids revolutionized the way portraits are approached.

Julie Mehretu

Abstract Painter

Julie Mehretu is a contemporary artist known for her large-scale, abstract works that blend mapping and architectural elements with references to history, politics, and geography.

Sheila Hicks

Textile Artist

Sheila Hicks is an American artist known for her pioneering work in fiber art. She has created large-scale installations and small woven pieces using a variety of materials.

Lisa Yuskavage

Painter

Lisa Yuskavage is an American painter celebrated for her provocative and psychologically charged figurative works that blend classical painting techniques with contemporary themes.

Jonathan Borofsky

Installation Artist

Jonathan Borofsky is an American contemporary artist known for his large-scale, multimedia installations that explore themes of humanity, consciousness, and the subconscious.
